# Student Engagement Analytics Consulting Project | Trinity Institutional Research

## Overview

This project was completed as part of my Consulting Experience in Business Analytics capstone in partnership with Trinity University's Office of Institutional Research & Effectiveness (TUIR).

Our team analyzed National Survey of Student Engagement (NSSE) data from 2022 and 2025 to identify how student engagement changes throughout the undergraduate experience and provide evidence-based recommendations for institutional improvement.

The objective was not only to perform statistical analysis, but to translate survey findings into actionable strategies for university leadership.

---

## Business Problem

Student engagement is strongly connected to retention, academic success, and graduation outcomes.

However, traditional institutional surveys provide snapshots of student experiences rather than explaining:

- How engagement changes over time
- Which experiences accelerate student development
- Whether interventions should differ across student groups

**Goal:** Use longitudinal student engagement data to identify high-impact opportunities for improving the undergraduate experience.

---

## My Contributions

As a primary analytical contributor, I worked on:

- Cleaned and reshaped NSSE survey datasets containing 1,000+ student responses across multiple survey years
- Prepared engagement variables across Academic Challenge, Learning with Peers, Experiences with Faculty, and Campus Environment dimensions
- Developed statistical analyses comparing engagement differences across Trinity's academic schools
- Conducted one-way ANOVA testing to evaluate whether Campus Environment and Faculty Experience perceptions differed by school
- Interpreted statistical findings and converted results into institution-level strategic recommendations
- Developed executive-facing presentation materials focused on insight communication and action planning

---

## Analytical Approach

Research methods included:

### Longitudinal Engagement Analysis
- Repeated-measures MANOVA
- Follow-up ANOVA testing
- Freshman-to-senior engagement comparison

### Experiential Learning Impact Analysis
- Mixed linear modeling
- High-impact practice participation analysis

### Academic School Comparison
- One-way ANOVA
- Cross-sectional segmentation analysis

---

## Key Findings

### Faculty engagement was the strongest improvement opportunity

Experiences with Faculty increased by +4.06 points from freshman to senior year, representing the largest positive engagement change.

### Experiential learning benefits occurred too late

Only 10.9% of freshmen participated in experiential learning opportunities compared with 71.4% of seniors, suggesting an opportunity to introduce these experiences earlier.

### Academic school differences were not significant

Campus Environment and Faculty Experience perceptions were statistically similar across Trinity's four academic schools, supporting university-wide solutions instead of separate school-specific programs.

---

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis, our team recommended:

- Expanding structured first-year faculty engagement opportunities
- Creating earlier experiential learning pathways
- Developing peer collaboration systems
- Building engagement KPIs and feedback loops between NSSE survey cycles
